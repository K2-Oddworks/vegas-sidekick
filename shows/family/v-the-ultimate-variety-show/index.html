What to Expect

🤸 World-Class Acrobatics
Aerial acts, hand balancing, tumbling, and strength feats from internationally trained performers. The production values are Las Vegas-grade — full lighting rigs, professional staging, acts that compete at the world level.
🪄 Magic & Illusion
Sleight-of-hand close-up magic and stage illusion performed by professionals who know how to work a crowd. V's magic acts tend toward the interactive — expect audience participation and moments that will genuinely fool you.
🎤 Stand-Up Comedy
Las Vegas-tested comedians who know how to read a room full of tourists on vacation. The comedy acts are some of V's most crowd-pleasing moments — clean enough for the family, sharp enough to make adults actually laugh.
🫙 Precision Juggling
Juggling acts that push well beyond the typical club-juggling showcase. Specialty props, tempo changes, multi-person passing routines. The juggling at V is the kind that makes the audience gasp.
⛸ Roller Skating
A fan-favorite signature act — inline roller skaters performing acrobatic moves at speed on a live stage. It's one of those things you have to see to fully believe is happening in front of you. Kids especially love this one.
💪 Something for Everyone
V is built for mixed groups — families, couples, corporate outings, friend groups. No single act goes long enough to lose anyone, and the variety format means there's guaranteed to be at least two or three things that become the highlights of someone's trip.

🪑 Sweet Spot
The closest seats in the house — VIP positions you at the front of V Theater, directly at the foot of the stage. In the cabaret-table arrangement, you're in the performer's immediate space. The comedy acts will almost certainly interact with you, the magic acts will use you, and the acrobats will perform right over your head. The trade-off: for aerial acts and roller skating, you'll be looking up at a steep angle and may miss some of the full-stage picture. Great for people who want maximum immersion and don't mind being part of the show.
Spike's pick for V. Preferred seats occupy the center rows (A–H) of V Theater — the heart of the house. From here you get the ideal viewing angle for every act type: close enough to feel the energy and catch performer detail, far enough to see the full stage picture when the acrobats form group compositions or the roller skaters build speed across the full floor. V is a format show — each act fills the whole stage differently — and Preferred gives you the universal sightline that works for all of them. For groups with kids, this is the seat that keeps everyone comfortable and engaged for the full 90 minutes.
Rows K and L at the rear of the main floor. You're further from the stage, but V's production is scaled to fill the room — the acts project well and the stage picture is complete from anywhere in the house. The distance does cost you some of the close-up detail and the immediate front-row energy, but GA remains a complete experience of the show. If budget is the primary consideration, GA is a solid choice — you'll see everything and have a great time.
