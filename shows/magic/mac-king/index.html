About the Show

About Mac King

Mac King has been performing the same one-man show in Las Vegas since January 2000 — first at Harrah's for two decades, now at Excalibur's Thunderland Theater. Over 10,000 shows to more than two million fans. The longest-running one-man show in Las Vegas history. When Penn & Teller had him on their show three times and called him the greatest comedy magician alive today, they weren't being polite. They were making a professional judgment about someone in their field who operates at a level few people reach.

🌵
Spike's Take
$33 for Mac King. MAGIC Magazine's #1 comedy magician in the world. Las Vegas Review-Journal named this the best bargain show on the Strip for eight straight years. They were right. Two shows daily at 1 PM and 3 PM means scheduling flexibility. The Thunderland Theater is purpose-built cabaret-style seating — every table has a clear view and drinks are available at your seat.

The show runs 70 minutes in Mac's signature style: close-up sleight of hand that happens in the audience's hands, large illusions, and comedy wrapped around every bit of it. The props are deliberately low-tech — a goldfish, Fig Newtons, a yellow raincoat he calls a "cloak of invisibility." That's a creative choice, not a budget constraint. It's what makes the show work for a five-year-old and a Magic Castle member simultaneously.

What You'll Experience

What to Expect

🎩 Close-Up Magic in Your Hands
Mac King's signature style puts the magic in the audience's hands — literally. The close-up work happens directly with guests in the room, making the impossible feel even more impossible when you're holding the prop.
😂 Comedy First, Magic Second
The plaid suit is the tell. Mac King is first a comedian who does magic, not a magician who tells jokes. Penn & Teller called him the greatest comedy magician alive. 70 minutes where neither element would work without the other.
🐟 The Props Are the Point
A goldfish. A box of Fig Newtons. A yellow raincoat. Deliberate choices — the low-tech props are what make the close-up magic more impossible. There's nowhere to hide, no special equipment to explain away.

About the Thunderland Theater
A purpose-built cabaret showroom — angled table seating with full cocktail service from every position. There isn't a bad angle in the room. Mac King's show has filled this type of intimate cabaret layout for 25 years because the format is exactly right for the show. Pick your section based on how close you want to be to the action.
The angled table banks on the left side of the Thunderland Theater. A diagonal perspective on the stage — you see Mac King's full performance frame, including audience interaction happening across the room. Cocktail service at every table. A solid section in a room designed so every seat works.
