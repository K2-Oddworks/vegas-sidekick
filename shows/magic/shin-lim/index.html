About Shin Lim

Shin Lim won America's Got Talent in Season 13. Then he came back for AGT: The Champions and won again — the only performer in the show's history to win twice. He holds multiple Guinness World Records for card manipulation, and his close-up sleight of hand technique is widely considered the most precise in the world. None of that fully prepares you for watching it happen three feet in front of you.

It lands differently than a stage spectacle. People walk out genuinely unsettled in the best possible way.

What to Expect

🃏 Close-Up Card Magic at World-Record Level
Shin Lim holds multiple Guinness World Records for card manipulation — the technical precision of what he does with a standard deck is objectively the best in the world. This is not stage illusion magic with trapdoors and assistants. It's sleight of hand happening at a speed and scale that shouldn't be possible. The Palazzo Theatre's intimacy makes that fact very difficult to deny.
🔇 Quiet. Precise. Intentional.
The show is deliberately understated compared to most Las Vegas productions. No pyrotechnics, no large-scale production spectacle, no filler. The silence is part of the performance — Shin Lim works without narrating what he's doing, which makes the impossible more impossible when it happens. People who come expecting a big Vegas production sometimes need a moment to recalibrate. Those who come ready to watch leave shaken in a good way.
🏆 Won AGT. Then Won Again.
America's Got Talent Season 13 winner. Then AGT: The Champions winner — the only performer in the show's history to do it twice. That's not a résumé detail; it's a data point about what happens when the largest general-audience panel in American television sees his work for the first time. Twice. The reaction both times was the same.
🚫 Leave the Camera Behind
Cameras are not permitted inside the Palazzo Theatre. Backpacks and large bags aren't either. Travel light — phone in your pocket, essentials only. The no-camera rule is enforced, and honestly, you won't need one. What Shin Lim does cannot be captured on a phone screen in a way that does it justice. Being present for it is the point.
