What to Expect

🧠 Mentalism, Not Stage Magic
The Mentalist is a fundamentally different kind of show. No cards, no assistants, no apparatus. Gerry McCambridge works with the audience directly — reading behavior, making predictions, revealing information that people haven't shared out loud. The format is pure mentalism: the effect is unsettling in the best way. You spend the drive back trying to reverse-engineer what happened and coming up empty.
🏠 Intimate Room, Every Seat Works
The V Theater upstairs (V2) is a small, intimate showroom. Usher seating means you'll be placed in your seat on arrival rather than choosing independently — and because the room is genuinely intimate with no bad angles, every position in the house delivers the full experience. Arrive before showtime and let the usher do the rest.
👥 The Audience Is the Show
Mentalism works because it's performed on real people in real time. The volunteers aren't plants, the information isn't staged, and the reactions you're watching are genuine. No two nights are identical because the audience changes everything. The intimate room amplifies this — you're close enough to watch the faces of the people being read, which is its own experience within the show.
🚫 No Cameras or Recording
The use of cameras and audio/video devices are strictly prohibited during the show. This is enforced. Stow your device and watch the performance — mentalism is easier to miss if you're half-watching through a screen. What McCambridge does demands your full attention to land the way it's supposed to.
