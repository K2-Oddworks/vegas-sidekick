What to Expect

🔍 Deductive Mentalism, Not Stage Magic
Colin Cloud's performance is built on deduction, not apparatus. He reads people — their backgrounds, their choices, their psychology — with a specificity and speed that made audiences and judges on Britain's Got Talent genuinely uncomfortable. He doesn't ask setup questions that lead you toward answers. He tells you things. That's a different mechanism from what most mentalism shows do, and the effect lands differently because of it.
⚡ Faster and Sharper Than You Expect
Colin's style is confrontational and fast. He doesn't build slowly — the deductions arrive quickly and they're specific. There's a precision to his mentalism that feels more like a detective interrogation than a stage show. If you've seen traditional mentalism and found it too slow or too theatrical, Mastermind runs differently. He hits fast and moves on before you've finished processing the previous reveal.
🏠 Intimate Cabaret — Every Seat Works
Harrah's Cabaret is a cozy showroom with three price levels — P1 up front, P2 mid-house, P3 toward the rear and sides. In a room this size, the mentalism reaches every section. P1 gives you the highest chance of being directly involved in a routine. Every other section delivers a full, unobstructed view of the show. There are no bad seats in this room.
📵 No Photos or Video
No photos or video during the show. Put the phone away once the performance begins. Colin Cloud's show is one where being present and alert matters — he's working the room actively and the full effect of the mentalism depends on your attention. Missing moments because you're filming means missing the thing that makes this show worth seeing.
