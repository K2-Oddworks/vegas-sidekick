About Nathan Burton Comedy Magic

Nathan Burton has been headlining on the Las Vegas Strip since 2001. That's more than two decades of nightly performances — a track record that doesn't happen without a show worth coming back to. Multiple seasons on America's Got Talent gave him a national profile, but the performance that had an estimated 1.4 billion viewers watching was a live appearance on China's Got Talent. The Strip career came first, and it's what's kept him here. Now he performs in his own purpose-built theater inside FlyOver Las Vegas, between Hard Rock Cafe and Ross on the Strip.

🌵
Spike's Take $28 for a 70-minute afternoon magic show in a room that was built for it — no bad angles, intimate enough that the close-up magic actually lands. The VIP Couches and VIP section are both the Sweet Spot here. If you've got kids or you need a show that works for everyone in the group without negotiation, this is the call.

The show covers the full range of magic: large-scale stage illusions at rapid-fire pace alongside close-up magic that happens directly in the audience's hands. Beautiful showgirls are part of the production. Nathan stays after every performance to meet the audience and take photos — a detail that matters more than it sounds when you have kids who just watched someone disappear in front of them. All ages, no restrictions, 4 PM every day except Thursday.

What to Expect

🎩 Large-Scale Illusions + Close-Up Magic
The show covers both ends of the magic spectrum. Large stage illusions run at rapid-fire pace while close-up magic happens directly in the audience's hands. The combination keeps the energy moving and the room engaged throughout the full 70 minutes — there isn't a slow section built into the format.
🎭 Comedy, Showgirls & Personality
This isn't a straight-faced prestige magic show. Nathan Burton's casual, genuinely funny stage presence is built into every act. Beautiful showgirls are part of the full production. The room is intimate enough that the comedy lands — you're close enough to see every reaction and every setup pay off.
📸 Meet & Greet After the Show
Nathan stays after every performance to meet the audience and take photos. If you've got kids who just watched someone vanish from a box in front of them, this is the moment that makes the day. It's not rushed — it's part of the show experience, not a VIP add-on.
📷 Check Before You Shoot
Still photos may be welcome — check with your usher before you pull out a camera. Flash photography is strictly prohibited. Video recording is not permitted. If an usher asks you to stop, put the phone away. This is enforced.
