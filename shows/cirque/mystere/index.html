About the Show

The Show That Started It All

Mystère opened at TI Hotel in 1993 — the first permanent Cirque du Soleil production ever built in Las Vegas. It predates "O," KÀ, and every other Cirque show on the Strip. That matters because Mystère is the one that proved the concept: that Las Vegas audiences would show up night after night for something genuinely theatrical, not just flashy. More than 30 years later it is still running, and it still earns its place.

The show is pure Cirque — no gimmicks, no celebrity tie-ins, no comedy subplot. Acrobatics, aerial acts, contortion, and stunning physical performance built around an abstract story of life, creation, and the mysteries of existence. The staging is intimate compared to KÀ or "O," which means every seat feels close and the performances feel personal rather than monumental.

Start here for Cirque. If you've never seen a Cirque du Soleil show, Mystère is exactly where to begin. It is the purest version of what Cirque actually is — before the aquatic stage of "O," before the massive battle platform of KÀ, before any of the theme concepts. From $44 on most dates. No age restrictions, no adult content, no prerequisites. Just the original, still running, still delivering.
