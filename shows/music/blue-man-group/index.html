About Blue Man Group

Blue Man Group has been performing at Luxor Las Vegas for over two decades, and it has not gotten stale. Three bald, blue-painted performers walk audiences through a show that combines live percussion, wordless physical comedy, audience participation, and technology-driven spectacle into something that defies easy categorization. It's genuinely original in a city full of production shows that follow familiar formulas.

The Blue Man Theater at Luxor was purpose-built for this production. What to Expect

🥁 Live Percussion
The drumming is the spine of the show. Blue Man Group plays percussion instruments in ways that shouldn't work as well as they do — some purpose-built, some industrial, some completely unrecognizable. The sound is physically present in the theater. This is not background music. The percussion sequences are the centerpiece, and they're legitimately impressive.
😂 Wordless Comedy
All comedy is physical and visual — no dialogue, no language barrier. The three performers communicate entirely through movement, reaction, and timing. The humor is consistently unexpected and never crude. It plays as well for kids as for adults, and it holds up across repeat viewings because the physical comedy is genuinely skilled.
🎯 Audience Participation
The audience is part of the show. Volunteers get pulled into acts throughout — not humiliating, not forced. The performers are skilled at making participation feel natural. If you are firmly against being selected, sitting higher in the 200 level lowers the odds. If you want to be part of it, sit closer to the stage in the 100 sections.
💥 Sensory Spectacle
Expect paint, paper, light, projection, and carefully orchestrated chaos. The show uses the full width and height of the stage. There are moments of genuine visual surprise throughout the 90 minutes — things that are difficult to describe and easy to experience. The production values are high and the staging has continued to evolve over the show's long Las Vegas run.
