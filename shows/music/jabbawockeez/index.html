White masks. White gloves. No faces. All the communication happens through movement.

What to Expect

🎭 World-Class Synchronized Dance
The Jabbawockeez built their reputation on a style of synchronized movement so precise it looks impossible. On stage at MGM Grand, with proper lighting and production, the effect is even more pronounced than on television. The formations, the timing, the small details in footwork that ripple across the whole crew simultaneously — this is what the show is built around, and it delivers.
😂 Comedy That Actually Lands
The Jabbawockeez are funny — not in a "Las Vegas comedy segment" way, but in a way that's integrated into the choreography and the visual storytelling. The white masks mean all communication is physical, which makes the comedic moments hit differently than they would with visible faces. Audience participation is real, not scripted awkward volunteer moments. Kids love it. Adults love it.
🎵 Music That Drives the Show
The Jabbawockeez show has always been about the relationship between movement and music — the production at MGM Grand takes that seriously. The soundtrack spans hip-hop, pop, and electronic, and every piece of choreography is built to it rather than around it. The sound system in the theater handles it properly. This isn't a dance recital with a playlist; it's a full musical production.
👥 Audience Participation Done Right
Audience participation in Las Vegas shows is usually a liability. In Jabbawockeez, it's a genuine highlight. The crew has been doing this long enough that the audience interaction moments are as polished as the choreography — they know how to make the room feel involved without making anyone feel singled out. It creates the feeling that the whole theater is part of the show.
