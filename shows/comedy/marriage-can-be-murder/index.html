About Marriage Can Be Murder

Marriage Can Be Murder is not dinner theater. The distinction matters. In a standard dinner theater show, you eat at a table while something happens on a stage in front of you. Here, the cast performs throughout the entire Masquerade Village venue at the Rio — at your table, across the room, at the next table over, during the two 8-minute breaks when you're actively gathering clues. The show is woven through the room, not contained to a stage. The dinner isn't the break between acts. The dinner is part of the show.

🌵
Spike's Take $95 for two hours, a four-course dinner, and a murder mystery where the cast comes to your table. Regular seating is the Sweet Spot here — not because R.I.P. or VIP aren't good, but because the show is so thoroughly spread throughout the room that every seat is essentially front row. Pick Regular, save money, get the same show.

The format runs two hours with two 8-minute breaks built in for mingling and clue-gathering — those breaks are part of the experience, not intermissions. A four-course dinner is included at every seating tier. The cast plays a married couple caught up in a murder, the audience plays investigator, and by the end of the evening someone at your table has probably developed a theory. It's the kind of show that works for date nights, group outings, and anniversaries equally well — and notably, for families with kids ages 8 and up.

What to Expect

🎭 The Show Comes to You
Multiple stages are positioned throughout the Masquerade Village venue, and the cast moves through the entire room. This is not dinner theater where action happens on a single stage at the front. Scenes play out at your table, the table beside you, and across the room. Every seat in the house is part of the show.
🔍 You're an Investigator
Two 8-minute breaks are built into the evening specifically for mingling and gathering clues. The murder mystery unfolds over the course of dinner — by the end of the night you'll have formed a theory, argued with your table, and almost certainly been wrong about at least one thing. The interactive element is the whole show, not an add-on.
🍽️ Dinner Is Part of the Show
A four-course dinner is included with every ticket. It's not an intermission between acts or a buffer before the show starts. The food and the mystery unfold simultaneously — scenes happen while you're eating, and the cast doesn't wait for your table to finish a course before the next plot point arrives.
📸 Photos Are Welcome
Feel free to take a few photos during the show — this is one of the few Vegas productions where the camera isn't a problem. Flash photography and video recording are not permitted. Put the phone down and be present for the actual show. You'll get more out of it.
