About Tape Face

He doesn't say a word. He doesn't need to. Tape Face — as seen on America's Got Talent — has built one of the most genuinely original shows on the Las Vegas Strip out of art, music, props, and pure physical comedy. Performing nightly at the Underground Theater inside MGM Grand, the show is enjoyed by audiences of every age, language, and background because it doesn't require any of them to have the same frame of reference. The comedy is universal in the most literal sense of the word.

🌵
Spike's Take This is the show you bring people to when you have no idea what kind of comedy they like. No language needed, no shared cultural context required, no adult content to navigate around. It just works on everybody in the room — which almost nothing else can say.

Described as a modern-day jester, Tape Face deploys an ever-rotating cast of characters, props, and stunts — each building on the last in ways the audience can't anticipate and won't forget. The show is consistently unpredictable because Tape Face works the room differently every night, pulling in the crowd and making each performance feel assembled specifically for the people in it. Seventy-five minutes that moves fast and lands harder than you'd expect from something with no dialogue.

What to Expect

🤐 Zero Words. All Show.
Tape Face doesn't speak — not a single line. Everything he communicates comes through physical comedy, music, props, and reaction. It's a genuinely impressive creative constraint that produces 75 minutes of comedy that works on literally everyone in the room, regardless of age or native language.
🎪 Props, Stunts & Characters
Tape Face cycles through an evolving cast of characters and situations — each built from physical comedy, unexpected props, and the kind of timing that takes years to develop. The show moves fast. You won't have time to see the next bit coming, which is exactly the point.
👥 Audience Interaction
The crowd is part of the show. Tape Face regularly pulls in audience members — not to embarrass them, but to build something funny with them. It's collaborative in a way that most Vegas shows aren't. The results depend on who shows up, which is part of what keeps it fresh every night.
📷 Check Before You Shoot
Still photos may be welcome at this show — check with your usher before you pull out a camera. Flash photography and video recording are not permitted. If an usher asks you to put the phone away, do it. This is enforced.
