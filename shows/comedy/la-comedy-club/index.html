About LA Comedy Club

The Dragon Room inside The STRAT Hotel Casino Tower runs three separate comedy shows every night of the week — and they're three genuinely distinct products. The 6 PM Happy Hour Variety Show is a lighter mix of stand-up and variety acts. The 8 PM show brings rotating touring comics. The 10 PM slot features named headliners: Bret Ernst on Tuesday and Wednesday, Butch Bradley Thursday through Monday. One venue, three different reasons to show up depending on the night and the time.

🌵
Spike's Take $43 for 75 minutes of stand-up in a room where both seating sections — VIP and GA — are legitimately good. The STRAT is north of the main Strip cluster, which keeps the crowd local-leaning and the vibe a little less tourist-adjacent. That's a feature, not a bug.

The Dragon Room is an intimate purpose-built comedy space with clear sight lines from both sections. VIP seats wrap tight to the stage, GA fills out behind them — and unlike a lot of rooms where the back rows feel disconnected, the Dragon Room is small enough that neither section is a bad seat. Check who's on the 10 PM bill before you book; Bret Ernst and Butch Bradley both have strong followings and different styles, so knowing which one is on stage matters.

What to Expect

🎤 Three Shows, Three Formats
This isn't one show running three times — each time slot is a different product. 6 PM is a lighter Happy Hour Variety Show. 8 PM brings rotating touring comics. 10 PM features a named headliner who changes by day of the week. Pick the time that matches what you want out of the night, and check the 10 PM bill before you commit.
🌙 The 10 PM Headliner
Bret Ernst runs Tuesday and Wednesday. Butch Bradley holds Thursday through Monday. Both are seasoned touring comics with serious credits — they're not filler acts. Knowing which one is on stage is worth a quick check before you book, especially if you're a fan of either.
🪑 Both Sections Are Good
The Dragon Room is intimate enough that VIP and GA both deliver a solid experience. VIP puts you close to the stage with the most immersive view. GA fills out behind it with clear sight lines throughout. Spike recommends both — pick based on preference and price, not on fear of a bad seat.
📵 Please, No Photos or Video
The club asks that guests leave the phones alone during the show — out of respect for the performers and everyone else in the room. It's a request, not a hard ban, but it's the right call. Put it away, be present, and watch the comedy. You'll have a better time for it.
