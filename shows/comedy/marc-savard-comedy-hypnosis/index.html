About Marc Savard

Prepare for one of the most chaotic, side-splitting experiences in Las Vegas. Marc Savard Comedy Hypnosis turns the spotlight on the audience — transforming everyday volunteers into the stars of a hilariously unpredictable 70-minute spectacle. From absurd dance battles to over-the-top characters and unbelievable hypnotic moments, no two performances are ever the same, because the hilarity depends on who steps onstage.

🌵
Spike's Take Nobody makes you do anything here. Raising your hand to volunteer is completely your call — and staying in your seat is just as valid. The Nathan Burton is small enough that you're part of the room either way.

For more than 15 years, Marc Savard has headlined on the Las Vegas Strip with sharp comedy, expert showmanship, and mind-bending hypnotic skill built across 7,500+ live performances and 30 years working full-time as a professional hypnotist. Whether you prefer to sit back and watch the madness unfold or jump onstage and take part yourself, every seat offers a front-row view of the insanity.

Wild, immersive, and endlessly entertaining — this is the kind of Vegas show people talk about long after the night ends.

What to Expect

🙋 Volunteering Is Optional
Marc opens the show by inviting anyone who's interested to raise their hand — that's the full extent of any pressure. If you step up, he guides you through everything. If you'd rather watch, you're still part of the show. A lot of people come specifically to watch other people get hypnotized and have just as good a night.
🎲 No Two Shows Are the Same
The volunteers are real audience members. Marc builds the entire show around whoever steps up — the characters, the scenarios, the comedy. There is no script because there can't be. Regulars who've seen this show dozens of times still say each visit hits differently.
🎤 Comedy Meets Hypnosis
Marc isn't just a hypnotist. He's a sharp comedian who plays the straight man to whatever the volunteers create on stage. The combination is rare — 30 years of technical expertise wrapped in genuine comedic instinct. The result is a room that doesn't stop laughing for 70 minutes.
