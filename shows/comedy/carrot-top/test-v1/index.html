The Show

Prop Comedy's Last Superstar

Scott Thompson — Carrot Top — has been headlining the Luxor for over 20 years. That's not a residency. That's a landmark. While other Vegas acts come and go, Carrot Top keeps selling out the Atrium Showroom six nights a week with a show that defies easy categorization.

The format is deceptively simple: a trunkful of custom props, a sharp political and pop-culture brain, and the kind of physical commitment you don't see from comedians who play it safe. Every prop has been engineered for a specific bit. Nothing is accidental.

Sidekick's Take: The center section is where this show lives. You want to be close enough to see the props clearly but far enough back to take in the full spectacle when 40 of them come out at once. Book center, sit back, and just let it happen.

The material skews topical — expect bits about whatever's in the news, gaming, Vegas itself, and a few recurring characters that regulars recognize. First-timers get the full intro. Repeat visitors get inside jokes.

Photography policy: No video, no flash photography during the show. Phone cameras (non-flash) are generally tolerated for static shots. Respect the room.

What to Expect

The Full Picture

The Props

Hundreds of custom-built, one-of-a-kind props developed over decades. Each one is purpose-built for a specific bit. You'll see things you couldn't have predicted.

The Topicality

Carrot Top follows the news obsessively. Expect bits about current events, pop culture, and whatever happened this week that he found absurd.

The Energy

High-intensity from the first minute. No warmup act, no slow build — Carrot Top opens at 100% and maintains it. The Atrium Showroom feels like a live event, not a show.

Crowd Interaction

Front rows will get the most direct attention. He works the room — expect some back-and-forth. Nobody gets humiliated, but nobody's invisible either.

The Venue

The Atrium Showroom is intimate by Vegas standards — a few hundred seats, no bad angle. The stage is visible from everywhere. Arrive early for parking; the Luxor lot is straightforward.

First-Timer Tips

Don't try to explain what you saw to someone who wasn't there. It won't make sense. Book the center section. Come with a drink. Don't overthink it.
