About Absinthe

Absinthe is a Spiegelworld production that has been running at the Spiegeltent on Caesars Palace's Roman Plaza since 2011 — making it one of the longest-running live shows in Las Vegas history. In that time it has built a reputation as the most acclaimed live entertainment experience on the Strip: TripAdvisor's top-rated Las Vegas show for over a decade, the show HuffPost famously declared "If you only see one show, make it Absinthe."

The format is deliberate and intimate. The Spiegeltent is a custom circular tent holding approximately 571 people, with a center stage and seating all the way around it. No seat is more than 12 rows from the action. World-class circus performers — aerialists, acrobats, strength artists, roller skate acts — perform genuine feats of athleticism at distances that would not be possible in a large theater. The Gazillionaire, the show's host and ringleader, connects every act with sharp adult humor that earns the show's 18+ classification. This is not a circus for families.

What to Expect

🤸 World-Class Circus Acts
Absinthe features genuinely elite performers — aerialists, acrobats, contortionists, roller skate artists, and strength acts drawn from world-class competitive backgrounds. These aren't theatrical performers doing circus-adjacent moves. They're athletes performing at the edge of what's physically possible, at distances you'd never see in a full arena. At 12 rows maximum, you will feel every act.
🎭 Sharp, Unfiltered Adult Comedy
The Gazillionaire — the show's flamboyant and spectacularly dressed host — delivers the kind of dark, raunchy adult humor that earns Absinthe its 18+ classification. The comedy is sharp, intentional, and earns big laughs. It also isn't for everyone, which is exactly why the show works: it commits completely. Don't bring guests who bruise easily.
🎪 An Intimate Circular Tent
The Spiegeltent is a custom structure built specifically for this show — not a hotel showroom, not a retrofitted theater. The circular design puts every seat close to the action. Performers use the full 360 degrees of the stage, which means something is happening near you throughout the show. The tent itself has an old-world European aesthetic that sets the tone from the moment you walk in.
🏆 Las Vegas' Most Acclaimed Show
Absinthe has been TripAdvisor's top-rated Las Vegas show for over a decade. It's been running since 2011 — longer than most shows that tried to follow it. The acclaim isn't hype: this is a show that has earned every review through consistent performance quality and a format that doesn't have a weak spot. The combination of real athleticism and genuine comedy in an intimate setting is something Vegas rarely manages to produce.

Frequently Asked

Absinthe is a circular tent show featuring world-class circus acts — aerialists, acrobats, roller skate artists, contortionists, and strength performers — hosted by a character called the Gazillionaire. The Gazillionaire delivers sharp adult comedy between acts, earning the show its 18+ classification. It's not a traditional circus and it's not a comedy club: it's something uniquely its own, which is why it has been the most acclaimed show in Las Vegas for over a decade.
Very strictly. Everyone entering the Spiegeltent must be at least 18 years old and must have a ticket — no exceptions. Bring a valid government-issued ID. Guests who appear under 30 should expect to show ID at the door. Do not purchase tickets for a group that includes anyone under 18 — they will not be admitted, and tickets are non-refundable.
The Spiegeltent is located on the Roman Plaza at Caesars Palace — the outdoor plaza area between the main Caesars Palace entrance and the Las Vegas Strip. It's a distinctive circular tent structure that's hard to miss once you're on the plaza. Approach from the Strip-facing side of Caesars Palace. Give yourself 20–30 minutes before showtime to park, navigate the resort, and find the tent.
Cameras and audio/video recording devices are not permitted during the performance. This is one of Spike's firmest recommendations: put the phone away for these 70 minutes. The acts in Absinthe are the kind that lose something when you're watching through a screen. You won't want the footage later as much as you'll want the memory of actually being there. This is a show designed to be witnessed in person.
No official dress code. Absinthe draws a mix of guests in everything from casual vacation wear to dressed-up date-night attire. The show itself has an elevated, theatrical feel that makes people want to dress for the occasion — but you won't be turned away in jeans. Comfortable footwear is practical since the Spiegeltent can fill up and you may be standing briefly before the show.
