About Zombie Burlesque

Somewhere in a decaying 1950s Las Vegas, the dead came back — and they brought a full production number with them.

Zombie Burlesque rewinds the clock to a 1950s America where the undead have taken over, and the President of the United States has struck a peace deal with the zombie hordes — live prisoners traded for a shaky truce. Instead of hiding from what's left of civilization, audiences are invited in to see the "lively" side of zombie culture: a nightclub full of the sexiest, most dead women in town, sharing the stage with skilled variety performers, sharp comedy, and a live band that keeps the whole thing moving.

It's part monster movie, part cabaret, and entirely in on the joke. Burlesque numbers, sketch comedy, and specialty acts rotate through 75 fast-moving minutes, all scored live rather than over a backing track — a detail that separates this from a lot of themed revues on the Strip.

What to Expect

🧟 A 1950s World Overrun by the Undead
The premise: a peace deal between the President and the zombie hordes, live prisoners as the price, and an invitation for the rest of us to see the "lively" side of zombie culture. It's played entirely for laughs — campy B-movie horror, not genuine scares.
💃 Burlesque, Variety & Big Laughs
Burlesque numbers share the stage with variety acts and sketch comedy, performed by a cast that's clearly having as much fun with the concept as the audience is. Sexy, silly, and self-aware in equal measure.
🎸 A Live Band Driving Every Number
Nothing here runs on a backing track. A live band scores the entire production, giving the music — and the comedy timing built around it — a rawer, more immediate energy than a lot of themed revues on the Strip.
🎭 75 Fast-Moving Minutes, No Filler
No intermission, no dead stretches — acts rotate quickly enough that the runtime never drags. A late-night pick that still gets you out with time for whatever's next.
