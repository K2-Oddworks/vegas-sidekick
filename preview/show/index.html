Blue Man Group has been performing at Luxor Las Vegas for over two decades, and it has not gotten stale. Three bald, blue-painted performers walk audiences through a show that combines live percussion, wordless physical comedy, audience participation, and technology-driven spectacle into something that defies easy categorization. It's genuinely original in a city full of production shows that follow familiar formulas.

The Blue Man Theater at Luxor was purpose-built for this production. Every seat has good sightlines, no seat is more than 70 feet from the stage, and the theater's design ensures the sound hits the way it's supposed to — and it hits hard. The percussion is physically loud. The comedy is entirely wordless and visual. Front rows in sections 102 and 103 get ponchos — the show has a few genuinely splatter-worthy moments.

The show has continued to evolve since it opened. The technology elements have grown more sophisticated over the years, and the production values are high. At $65 through Vegas Sidekick, it's one of the better-value production shows on the Strip — particularly for groups with mixed ages or international travelers for whom language isn't a barrier.

Yes. Blue Man Group is genuinely all-ages — no dialogue means no language barrier or adult content. Children under 4 are admitted free on a lap. The show is recommended for ages 3+ because the percussion is physically loud. Kids sensitive to loud sound may find it overwhelming — the 200 level is slightly farther from the speakers than the front of the lower sections if that's a concern.
The front rows of sections 102 and 103 receive ponchos before the show. There are moments where material is projected toward the audience and the front rows are genuinely in range. If you prefer to watch the splatter from a distance, the 200-level mezzanine is outside the splash zone. If you want to be in it, sit lower front — the ponchos cover you well.
Spike recommends the 200 level — specifically section 203 for center-stage perspective. Blue Man Group is a wide show and the production is designed to use the full horizontal stage. From the mezzanine you see everything happening left to right. The 100 sections are more immersive and up-close, but you lose the full scope. If it's your first time, sit in the 200s.
