Enter

You are entering a familiar place.

A mid-century resort that never stopped believing in itself — boomerang concrete, diamond-lattice glass, a five-pointed star worked quietly into the stone. It looks like it opened in 1963 and simply never let itself go soft.

Every room here belongs to someone. Look closely enough, and you'll find them.

01 — The Robin Suite

Living Room

for the one who loves horses, birds, and bright color

Coral velvet against teal, a checkerboard rug underfoot, a running horse rendered in oil above the credenza. A brass heron watches from the shelf. This room doesn't ask you to relax — it just quietly makes it impossible not to.

05 — Arrival

The Lobby

look up at the clock. now look again.

Everything here is beautiful and correct, mostly. The lattice screen above the desk throws a shadow more intricate than its own geometry should allow. Nobody's ever complained. Most people never notice.

06 — The Atrium

The Mechanism

not glitzy. not exciting. just watch it.

Dozens of brass rings, each turning at its own unrelated speed, four stories tall, lit from within. No plaque explains it. No one's asked for one. People sit down to check their phone for a minute and stand up forty minutes later.

07 — Poolside

The Pool

no DJ. no line. just quiet.

The water sits dark and still enough to mirror the sky rather than compete with it. Cabanas spaced far enough apart to matter. Joshua trees where a splash pad would usually be. It's the one place on property built entirely for daylight.

Open Sunrise to Sunset
a word before you go

Every hotel claims a story. This one just happens to be true — three people, one building, and a name borrowed from a man who understood that the strangest things are the ones hiding in plain sight.
