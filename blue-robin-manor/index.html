Enter

Somewhere between the paddock and the Strip.

Fog rolls across bluegrass lawns at sunrise. A horse stands watch over the fountain. By nightfall, the same grounds hum with warm light and good company — because this is still Las Vegas, and the sun does go down eventually.

Arrival

The Grounds

still enough to hear the fountain

A bronze horse stands quiet in the roundabout, water gently overflowing at his feet. Paddock fencing lines the drive. Behind it all, a tower rises the way the Strip insists it should — string lights, skyline, and just enough fog to remind you where the morning came from.

Blue Robin Manor — Resort & Grounds
A Three-Room Suite

The Loui Suite

Named for a small, gentle Yorkie who's seen enough of the world to know exactly where the afternoon sun lands. Three quiet rooms, and not one of them in a hurry.

Living Room

for an old friend who's earned the good chair

A leather chair, a warm fire, and a little bed tucked right into that patch of light. This is where he'd spend the whole afternoon — and you're welcome to do the same.

Bedroom

a proper Sunday nap, any day of the week

Soft caramel and dusty blue, low to the ground and easy to climb onto. The garden just outside the window does most of the talking.

Bath

a small paw print, if you look

Brass hardware carries his mark quietly on every drawer. The tub looks straight out over the gardens — the kind of soak that isn't going anywhere fast.

A Three-Room Suite

The Levi Suite

Named for a small black rescue with a mind of his own — a little guarded at the door, and entirely worth the wait. Give each room a minute; it warms up, same as he does.

Living Room

a little guarded at the door — worth the wait

The entry's composed, almost cool — but follow the light back to the corner and the room opens right up: a mismatched gallery wall, a well-loved leather chair, and a brass dog lamp that's clearly the one in charge.

Bedroom

a little eclectic, entirely on purpose

Charcoal and mustard, mixed patterns that don't quite match and don't need to. A small brass dog keeps watch from the dresser.

Bath

warms up, same as he does

Composed near the door, golden by the tub. Give it a minute. It's worth it.

The Corvette Bay

Art's Garage

a career, a bike, and a very good car

Doors thrown wide open to the sunshine, string lights overhead, the '65 parked like it's about to be driven right out into the afternoon. A lifetime with Coca-Cola on the wall. A motorcycle by the door. This one's a celebration, through and through.

Wine Bar

The Paddock

Derby hats, minus the fuss

Big color, big flowers, a wine wall that means business. The hats hang overhead as pure shape and shade — coral, marigold, lavender — because a room can be dressed up without taking itself too seriously.

house specialty: Mom's Biscuits and Gravy

Coffee's twenty-five cents, same as it should be. The jukebox plays what it wants to. And somewhere near the register, a small collage of two-dollar bills — a private joke between a mother and her son, hiding in plain sight.

The Michael Royce Study

no drama, just a good place to sit

The first thing every guest sees — a real library wall, warm light, nothing shouting for attention. Simple, steady, and exactly as much as it needs to be. Some people don't need a loud room to be remembered in.

A Bird-Watching Garden

Gmiter Shore Garden

The quiet heart of the manor — named in tribute, and given over to the birds. Mist rises off the water at first light; lavender and river stone line the path; and the feeder is never empty. A place built for slow mornings and long, unhurried looks at small winged things.
