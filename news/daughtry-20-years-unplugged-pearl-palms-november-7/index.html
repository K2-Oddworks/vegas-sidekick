Daughtry are coming to Las Vegas — and they're doing it differently this time. The multi-GRAMMY®-nominated, multi-platinum rock band has announced 20 Years Unplugged, a special acoustic tour that brings two decades of songs into more intimate spaces this fall. The Las Vegas stop lands at The Pearl Concert Theater at Palms Casino Resort on Saturday, November 7, 2026 at 8 p.m.

Presales run throughout the week ahead of general on sale, which begins Friday, June 19 at 10 a.m. PT. Tickets start at $45 (plus applicable fees) and are available at AXS.com. Support for the evening comes from Buffalo, NY singer-songwriter Ryan Perdz.

Twenty Years, Stripped Down

20 Years Unplugged doesn't just rework Daughtry's catalogue — it reframes it. These are songs built around the power of a voice and the weight of a lyric, and an acoustic setting lets all of that land without anything to hide behind. The scraped guitar string, the quiet before a chorus, the pause before the emotional gut-punch that Daughtry has always delivered — in a room like The Pearl, those moments are going to hit differently.

The tour follows the release of SHOCK TO THE SYSTEM (DELUXE), the complete two-part project via Big Machine Rock, which includes additional live recordings and tracks like "ARTIFICIAL," "PIECES," "THE BOTTOM," and "ANTIDOTE." The deluxe edition captures the band in one of their most direct, emotionally raw creative eras — and that energy should translate into something special in an intimate acoustic setting.

"Daughtry acoustic in a room the size of The Pearl is the kind of show that people are going to remember for a long time. These songs have always been about the voice — put Chris Daughtry in a smaller room with a guitar and you remove every layer between him and the audience. That's a rare thing."

— Kris Kidd, Vegas Sidekick

The Venue: The Pearl Concert Theater

The Pearl Concert Theater at Palms Casino Resort is one of the best mid-size music venues in Las Vegas — a 2,500-seat space designed with sightlines and acoustics in mind. Whether you're on the floor or in the elevated seating, you're close to the stage. For an acoustic show, that intimacy matters enormously, and The Pearl is one of the few rooms in Las Vegas where the experience scales to the music rather than the other way around.

Two Decades of Hits

Daughtry's run is genuinely remarkable. Their self-titled debut was the top-selling album of 2007 and the fastest-selling rock debut in SoundScan history, earning four GRAMMY® nominations and winning four American Music Awards. Eleven million albums sold. Twenty-five million singles. Two No. 1 debuts on the Billboard 200. Recent singles "World On Fire," "Heavy Is The Crown," and "Changes Are Coming" all cracked the Top 10 at Billboard Rock Airplay, and their 2023 cover of Journey's "Separate Ways (Worlds Apart)" with Lzzy Hale of Halestorm added another landmark to an already stacked catalogue.

In 2024, the band entered a heavier new chapter with SHOCK TO THE SYSTEM, earning three No. 1 singles — "ARTIFICIAL," "PIECES," and "THE BOTTOM." That arc from anthemic debut to emotionally raw new era is exactly what 20 Years Unplugged promises to cover.

Supporting Act
Ryan Perdz

The Buffalo, NY singer-songwriter and multi-instrumentalist brings a raw, guitar-driven voice to a new generation of modern rock. Raised on his grandparents' farm and citing Chris Cornell, John Frusciante, and Kurt Cobain as influences, Perdz recently announced his debut album Can't Cry (out August 14 via Big Machine Rock) and has been building momentum with singles "Sour," "Heavy Eyes," and a reimagining of MGMT's "Time to Pretend."
