A Country Legend on NFR Weekend

NFR week in Las Vegas is unlike anything else. The city transforms into the country music capital of the world for ten days every December, and this year Clay Walker joins the conversation in style. The Venetian Theatre is one of the most intimate mid-size venues on the Strip — 1,500 seats where every row feels close to the stage — making it an ideal setting for an artist whose catalog runs 30-plus years deep.

Walker's latest album Texas To Tennessee hit No. 1 on the iTunes Country Albums chart, drawing from a lineage of legends — George Jones, James Taylor, Bob Seger. His February single "Cowboys in Heaven" captures the reflective quality of a career that's given the genre 11 No. 1 hits. He recently sold out Ryman Auditorium for the first time, and he's playing major festivals throughout 2026 including Tortuga and Boots in the Park before closing out the year in Vegas.

Beyond the music, Walker has been one of the most outspoken advocates for Multiple Sclerosis awareness since his own diagnosis in 1996. His annual Clay Walker Charity Classic at Pebble Beach raises funds for MS research each year. A member of the Texas Cowboy Hall of Fame, his story is as compelling as his catalog.
