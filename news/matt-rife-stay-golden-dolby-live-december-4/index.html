The Record Books

The 2026 leg of Stay Golden is an extension of last year's run, which already rewrote the record books at arenas across the country. In New York, Rife played two sold-out nights at Madison Square Garden, becoming the youngest comedian in history to sell out the venue. That alone would be a career highlight for most. For Rife, it's one line on a much longer list.

  • Most single-event tickets ever sold at Nationwide Arena in Columbus
  • All-time ticket sales record at Climate Pledge Arena in Seattle
  • All-time ticket sales record at SNHU Arena in Manchester
  • Paid seat record at Van Andel Arena in Grand Rapids
  • Most tickets sold by a comedian in the history of Honda Center in Anaheim
600K+

Tickets sold on the ProbleMATTic World Tour — 250+ shows across North America, Europe, the UK, and Australia. All in under 48 hours following the 2023 announcement.

The ProbleMATTic tour established Rife as one of the fastest-growing acts in comedy history. Stay Golden has continued that trajectory without slowing down. This isn't a social media comedian doing a victory lap. The venues, the records, and the numbers tell a more serious story.

"Matt Rife didn't earn this crowd on late-night appearances. He built it on TikTok, then backed it up with some of the biggest comedy ticket numbers in arena history. Two nights at Madison Square Garden as the youngest comedian to ever do it. When that guy comes to Vegas, you book the ticket before the conversation ends."
Kris Kidd — Founder, Vegas Sidekick. Entertainment industry veteran.

The Netflix Special

Rife's latest Netflix special premiered globally on December 2, 2025. Matt Rife: Unwrapped — A Christmas Crowd Work Special was directed by Erik Griffin and taped in Phoenix. It explores "gifts, traditions, and who's made it on the naughty or nice list this year." Rife executive produced alongside Christina Shams, with production from his company Carpe Noctem, Avesta Entertainment, and Above Average.

It's the latest chapter in a Netflix relationship that has helped bring his crowd work — the raw, unscripted back-and-forth with audiences he built his following on — to a global audience. The Vegas crowd on December 4 will get the live version.

Inside Dolby Live

Dolby Live at Park MGM seats approximately 5,200 and is one of the most acoustically advanced venues on the Strip.

Dolby Live is one of the genuinely great rooms on the Strip. At around 5,200 capacity, it's an intimate setting relative to the arenas Rife has been filling, which means better sight lines, better sound, and a crowd that's actually close to the stage. If you've been to an outdoor amphitheater show this summer and felt miles from the performer, this is the correction.
