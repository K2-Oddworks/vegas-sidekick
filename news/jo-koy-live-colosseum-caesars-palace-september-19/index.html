Record-breaking comedian Jo Koy is returning to Las Vegas for one night only. He's headlining The Colosseum at Caesars Palace on Saturday, September 19, 2026, with an 8 p.m. show that's shaping up to be one of the comedy events of the year. Presales run throughout the week; general on sale kicks off Friday, June 19 at 10 a.m. PT at Ticketmaster.com.

This is a homecoming. Jo Koy built his career in this city — performing in Vegas coffee houses long before anyone was handing him arena headliner slots. Now he's playing one of the most legendary rooms in Las Vegas history, and doing it as one of the top ten grossing stand-up comedians on the planet.

Caesars Palace Las Vegas — home to The Colosseum, one of the most iconic entertainment venues in the world

About Jo Koy

Jo Koy is one of today's premier stand-up comedians, rising from performing in a Las Vegas coffee house to selling out arenas worldwide. His relatable comedy, drawn from his colorful family and Filipino heritage, has connected with audiences across cultures, making him one of the top 10 grossing stand-up comedians each year according to Pollstar and Billboard.

In 2023, Koy inked a two-special deal with Netflix. His first special under that deal, Jo Koy: Live From Brooklyn — filmed at the historic King's Theatre — premiered globally on June 4, 2024, and became the #6 most viewed special that year, with over 240 million minutes watched. A second Netflix special, filmed in Stockton, CA, is set to release later this year.

On Screen

Beyond stand-up, Jo Koy starred in the 2022 Universal Pictures comedy Easter Sunday, produced by Steven Spielberg's Amblin Partners, inspired by his life and Filipino roots. He's also appeared in Disney's Haunted Mansion, voiced characters in Netflix's Monkey King and Leo, and starred in Paramount+'s The Tiger's Apprentice.

Making History at SoFi

On March 21, 2026, Jo Koy and Gabriel "Fluffy" Iglesias became the first comedians to headline a sold-out comedy show at SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles for their One Night Only! event — one of the biggest comedy milestones in recent memory.

"Jo Koy has been one of the most in-demand comedians in the world for years, but he's never lost the feeling that he's doing this for the people in the room. He started playing Vegas coffee houses before any of this, and now he's headlining The Colosseum at Caesars Palace. That room has hosted legends. He fits right in."

— Kris Kidd, Vegas Sidekick
