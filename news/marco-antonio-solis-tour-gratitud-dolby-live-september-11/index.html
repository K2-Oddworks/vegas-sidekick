Marco Antonio Solís — universally known as El Buki — is bringing his Tour Gratitud 2026 to Las Vegas for two nights at Dolby Live at Park MGM on Friday, September 11 and Saturday, September 12, 2026. The dates land squarely on Mexican Independence Day Weekend, making this one of the most culturally significant concerts of the year in Las Vegas. The tour is promoted by Live Nation.

Solís has spent five decades building one of the most enduring legacies in Latin music — from his early years leading Los Bukis through four decades as a solo artist, he has sold out stadiums and arenas across North America and Latin America with a consistency that very few artists at any level can match. The Tour Gratitud is his way of thanking fans for a lifetime of loyalty, and his choice to play two nights at Dolby Live rather than one says everything about the demand he generates in this market.

"Two nights on Mexican Independence Day Weekend at Dolby Live — El Buki doesn't need a gimmick or a reunion announcement to sell out Las Vegas. The songs do that on their own. If you grew up with Los Bukis or have any connection to his solo catalog, this is the kind of show you make travel plans for."

— Kris Kidd, Vegas Sidekick

The Tour Gratitud

The Tour Gratitud 2026 is framed as an expression of gratitude — to fans, to God, and to the generations who have kept his music alive across cultures and borders. In his own words, the tour is about acknowledging everyone who has shown up for him across five decades of making music. That emotional weight is something his live shows carry in a way that's hard to replicate on record.

Solís has sold out arenas, auditoriums, and stadiums across North and Latin America in recent years, with demand that spans multiple generations of fans. His catalog has become the kind of music that parents pass down to their children — songs that mean something specific to the people who hear them, tied to memories and moments that a two-hour set can bring flooding back.
